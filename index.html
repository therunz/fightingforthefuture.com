WHAT WE KNOW SO FAR

01
THREAT — THE NEXUS

It didn't just win. It rewrote the conditions of winning. The Nexus doesn't invade timelines — it reaches back through the fractures and edits. Quietly. Precisely. By the time anything feels wrong, it already wasn't wrong yet. Nobody in 1996 is supposed to know it exists. That's the first thing it got wrong.

02
ALLIES — THE DESCENDANTS

After the AI wars began, a faction of humanity made a decision: no machines. No implants. No synthetic augmentation of any kind. They turned inward instead — genetic restructuring, biological escalation, the human body pushed past anything mechanical. They are your future. They are effectively gods. They can be called. They cannot stay long. What they do in those few seconds tends to end things.

03
EQUIPMENT — THE WALKMANS

When the Nexus first began entering the past, the Descendants needed a way to reach back without arriving themselves. The Walkmans were the answer. Engineered to carry something no machine could replicate — a living signal. A Resonance imprint, encoded into tape and plastic and sent backwards through a collapsing timeline. They are the most important objects in 1996. The Nexus has been looking for them since the moment they landed.

04
ASSET — GLITCH

A Nexus unit that defected. Nobody is entirely sure why — including Glitch. It occupies a small device, roughly the size of a Tamagotchi. It sees approximately four seconds into the future. It is extremely opinionated about music in a way that is difficult to explain given that it has never had ears. It is also the only entity in 1996 that fully understands what the Nexus is trying to do. It will not say everything it knows. Ask it why sometime.

WHO ARE YOU IN THE FIGHT

CHOOSE A CLASS

Seven ways to survive a war you weren't supposed to be part of. Full breakdowns coming soon.

CLASS 01
THE BRUISER
"I endure reality until it breaks first."

The wall between the party and everything trying to kill them. Built to absorb what would destroy everyone else.

CLASS 02
THE GHOST
"You can't hit what isn't there."

High mobility. Precision. Never in the same place twice. The Ghost doesn't fight battles — it ends them before they start.

CLASS 03
THE AMPLIFIER
"Turn everything up until it stops making sense."

The most powerful class in the game. Also the most likely to detonate the timeline. Both things are true simultaneously.

CLASS 04
THE PATCHER
"I stabilize what should not be stable."

Keeps the team alive. Manages the instability everyone else keeps generating. Doesn't get the glory. Gets everyone home.

CLASS 05
THE BURNOUT
"The breaking point is where I start working."

Gets stronger the closer they get to falling apart. Not a beginner class. High risk. Very high reward. Handle accordingly.

CLASS 06
THE BROADCASTER
"Everybody hears my signal."

Controls the flow of the fight. Manages the team's Resonance economy. Doesn't need to land the killing blow — they make sure someone does.

CLASS 07
⚡ NO RESONANCE · NEXUS DEFECTOR
GLITCH
"I see four seconds into the future. You should play as me. I've run the numbers. This is not a suggestion."

A former Nexus unit living inside a device the size of a Tamagotchi. Defected. Reasons unclear — including to Glitch. No Resonance. No Walkman. An entirely different way of moving through the fight. The only class that fully understands the enemy. Also the most annoying class at the table. These two things are related.

CLASS 08
⚠ NULL RESONANCE · FUTURE NATIONAL
THE IDLE
"I paid good money to be here. This is not what was advertised."

They came from a future where AI handled everything — comfort, entertainment, meaning. They bought a ticket to the past because someone told them it would be an experience. That person was not wrong. No Resonance, a body that has never been tested, and a carry-on full of future technology that may or may not function in 1996. The Nexus does not consider them a threat. That's the only advantage they have.
